📌 Quick Reference — Suspension Thresholds

🟢 Fully Licensed (Class G) Drivers
2–5
Warning letter from MTO urging improvement.
6–8
Second warning. At 8 pts: mandatory $50 demerit interview at ServiceOntario.
9–14
Possible suspension + mandatory interview. Miss it = suspended.
15+
30-day suspension. After: reduced to 7 pts (stay on record 2 yrs).
🟠 Novice Drivers (G1 / G2 / M1 / M2)
2–5
Warning letter from MTO.
6–8
Possible suspension + mandatory interview.
9+
60-day suspension. After: reduced to 4 pts.
Any 4+ pt offence
Escalating sanctions: 30-day (1st), 90-day (2nd), licence cancelled (3rd).
🧠 Memory System

Mnemonic tricks and visual anchors to lock in every number — the most tested topic on the G1 exam.

🔑 Master Framework: "The Ladder of Danger"

Think of demerit points as a ladder of danger: 2 → 3 → 4 → 6 → 7.

"2=Minor · 3=Moderate · 4=Serious · 6=Severe · 7=Maximum (hit & run)"

7
demerit points
  • Failing to remain at scene of collision (hit & run)
🧠 "7 is the ONLY one — and the WORST one."
6
demerit points
  • Careless driving
  • Racing / Stunt driving
  • Speed 50+ km/h over
  • Failing to stop for school bus
  • Failing to stop for police
  • Distracted driving (2nd/3rd+)
🧠 "6 = SCHOOL BUSES, POLICE, SPEED DEMONS."
4
demerit points
  • Speed 30–49 km/h over
  • Following too closely
  • Not stopping at pedestrian crossover
🧠 "4 = FTP: Following, Too-fast (30–49), Pedestrian crossover."
3
demerit points
  • Speed 16–29 km/h over
  • Distracted driving (1st)
  • Through/around railway barrier
  • Failing to yield right-of-way
  • Failing to obey stop sign/light
  • Improper passing
🧠 "3 = DISOBEDIENCE offences. Phone, passing, stop signs, yield."
2
demerit points
  • HOV lane misuse
  • Failing to lower headlight beam
  • Prohibited turns
  • Towing people (skis, bikes)
  • Improper turn / Failing to signal
  • Unnecessary slow driving
  • Reversing on highway
  • Child not in car seat
🧠 "2 = minor 'should know better' violations."
The "3-4.5-7-9.75" Speed Fine Formula

• 1–15 over → $3/km (0 pts)
• 16–29 over → $4.50/km + 3 pts
• 30–49 over → $7/km + 4 pts
• 50+ over → $9.75/km + 6 pts + stunt driving risk

Rule: fine rate goes up, demerit points go up — perfectly paired.

🚌 School Bus — "6-400-20"

6 demerit pts · $400 min fine (1st) · Stop 20 m away
2nd offence: $1,000–$4,000 + possible 6 months jail.

📱 Distracted Driving — "3 → 6 → 6"

1st: 3 pts + $615–$1,000  ·  2nd: 6 pts + $615–$2,000  ·  3rd+: 6 pts + $615–$3,000 + 6-month suspension

🚗 Suspension — "15 / 9 Split"

Full G → 15 pts → 30-day suspension → reduced to 7 pts
Novice → 9 pts → 60-day suspension → reduced to 4 pts

🍺 BAC — "0 · 5 · 8 = N · W · C"

0.00% = Novice (G1/G2) + all under-22  ·  0.05% = Warn range  ·  0.08% = Criminal

📏 Key Distances — "The Countdown"

150 m headlights · 30 m signal/no-pass zone · 20 m school bus · 9 m intersection · 5 m railway · 3 m hydrant · 2 m streetcar
150 → 30 → 20 → 9 → 5 → 3 → 2

🚫 "No Demerit Points But Big Fines" Traps

• No insurance: $5,000–$50,000 (0 pts)
• Impaired driving: Criminal Code — no HTA demerit pts
• Camera tickets: fine only, no pts
Exam trap → "Does no insurance add points?" → NO.
